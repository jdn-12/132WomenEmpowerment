This is our project entitled, "Women or We Men?", which focuses solely on women and their journey towards empowerment. We aim to analyze trends in indicators of women's empowerment, such as labor force participation and contraceptive use, across Asian countries. Our objective is to provide evidence-based insights into the challenges and opportunities for women's empowerment in diverse contexts.

Background

‘Shoot the vagina. No vagina, no use.’

These were the words uttered by President Rodrigo Duterte during a gathering with former New People’s Army members last 2018. For many, Duterte’s words epitomized the deeply-rooted gender inequality that continues to plague Philippine society. His remark served as a stark reminder of the persistent challenges faced by women, day by day. While Duterte’s words may have sparked controversy, they also ignited a broader conversation about advancing gender equality and empowering women to participate fully in all aspects of society. But do they actually get to? In this study, we aim to delve into the indicators of women’s empowerment—labor job participation and contraceptive use, and see if there has been progress at fostering greater gender equality.

Problem

Sure, there are many laws made to protect women's rights.

But despite strides in legislative measures, the quest for genuine defiance against gender discrimination and inequality remains a challenge worth further exploration. These questions might help in shedding light on this issue:

1. How has women's labor force participation in the Philippines changed over the years and how does it compare to other Asian countries?

2. What is the correlation between labor force participation and contraceptive use among women aged 15+ across Asian countries?

Solution

Our proposed solution leverages the power of data science to navigate social challenges, offering actionable insights for addressing disparities in gender inequality across Asian countries. Through statistical analysis, our findings will not only provide evidence-based guidance for policymakers but also pave the way for targeted interventions aimed at empowering women in diverse Asian contexts. .

Null Hypotheses

The data will not show any particular trend in recent years with regards to women’s labor force participation. The Philippines will not significantly differ from other countries in women’s labor force participation.

The data will show no significant correlation between women’s labor force participation and contraceptive use.

Alternative Hypotheses

The data will show a clear trend in recent years with regards to women’s labor force participation. The Philippines will significantly differ from other countries in women’s labor force participation.

The data will show a correlation between women’s labor force participation and contraceptive use.

Data Source

Information on labor force participation and contraceptive usage of women–expressed as a percentage of the population–was obtained from the World Bank's ‘World Development Indicators’ dataset. The dataset was compiled by the World Bank from data provided by the International Labour Organization (ILO) and the United Nations Population Division.

Data Pre-processing

A lot of the data, particularly in the labor force participation dataset, are either country-reported or missing, making it difficult to compare statistics internationally. To get a complete set of internationally comparable statistics, each dataset uses historical estimates in place of the missing data points.

The labor force participation dataset uses ILO modeled estimates, containing both reported observations and imputed data for countries with missing data. These estimates are produced through a series of econometric models maintained by the ILO and constantly revised to take into account new data. The estimates were available for the years 1991 through 2023.

The contraceptive usage dataset, on the other hand, did not have its missing data points already imputed. Thus, in order to maintain a consistent time series between the two datasets, the group imputed the missing values in the years between 1991 and 2023 through linear interpolation.

Finally, given that the scope of this project only covers Asian countries, all entries for regions not inside Asia were dropped from the dataset.
